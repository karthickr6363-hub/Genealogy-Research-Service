Irish Heritage Migration Research 12 generations • 3 countries

The Vanishing Meyer Surnames

When the Meyer family approached us, they had hit a 20-year brick wall trying to trace their German ancestry. Their great-grandfather had arrived in America in 1882, but all records seemed to end at Ellis Island.

The Breakthrough

Our researchers discovered that the family name had been anglicized from "Meier" to "Meyer" during processing at Ellis Island. By cross-referencing ship manifests with German parish records from Baden-Württemberg, we traced the family back to 1642.

Duration: 18 months
Documents: 47 verified
Countries: Germany, USA, Canada
Living Relatives: 12 found

"Finding our German roots after all these years has been life-changing. We even connected with cousins we never knew existed in Stuttgart!"

— Robert Meyer, Chicago, IL

Japanese Heritage DNA Analysis 6 generations • 2 countries

The Samurai's Daughter

The Tanaka family knew their grandfather had immigrated from Japan in 1885, but family stories suggested a noble past that seemed impossible to verify. Traditional records were scarce, and the family had nearly given up hope.

The Discovery

Through DNA analysis combined with meticulous research in Japanese feudal archives, we discovered that the family descended from a minor samurai clan from Kyoto. The migration to Hawaii's sugar plantations in 1885 was part of a larger pattern of noble families seeking new opportunities after the Meiji Restoration.

Duration: 14 months
Documents: 31 verified
Countries: Japan, USA
DNA Matches: 8 relatives

"Learning about our samurai heritage has given our family a profound sense of identity and connection to Japanese history we never knew we had."

— Mary Tanaka, Honolulu, HI

African Heritage Adoption Research 4 generations • 3 countries

The Scholar's Legacy

Jennifer Rodriguez was adopted as an infant and had spent decades searching for her biological roots. With limited information and sealed adoption records, her case seemed impossible—until DNA testing opened new doors.

The Connection

Advanced DNA analysis connected Jennifer to the Mensah family from Ghana. Through collaboration with local researchers, we traced her biological mother's educational journey from colonial Ghana to London in the 1960s. The discovery revealed a family tradition of scholarship spanning four generations.

Duration: 16 months
Documents: 18 verified
Countries: Ghana, UK, USA
Family Reunited: Yes, 2023

"Finding my biological family and learning about our educational legacy has been the most profound experience of my life. I finally feel complete."

— Jennifer Rodriguez, Miami, FL

French Heritage Military Records 8 generations • 2 countries

The Silent Weaver of Colmar

The Schmidt family had always heard stories about a French ancestor who was a revolutionary soldier, but no records could be found. The family had nearly given up on verifying this piece of their history.

The Revelation

Our researchers discovered that the family name had been changed from "Schmidt" to the French "Schmidt" during the French Revolution. Through careful analysis of lost parish records in Alsace, we uncovered the story of Jean-Pierre Schmidt, a weaver who fought in the revolutionary army and later established a textile business that survived the turmoil.

Duration: 12 months
Documents: 23 verified
Countries: France, USA
Business Legacy: Still operating

"Discovering our French revolutionary ancestor and the textile business still run by distant cousins in Paris has been absolutely incredible."

— Marie Schmidt, New York, NY
